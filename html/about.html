Got Trash?

Every year, in the United States alone, the average person generates about 5 pounds of solid waste. That means about 300 million tons end up in our landfills, and in addition, bacteria growing in packed, decomposing landfills give off methane gas, a contributor to increasing carbon dioxide levels in the air.

Fifteen percent of landfill waste is actually reusable, recyclable, or compostable. What can we do to help our planet and decrease the amount waste going to our landfills every year? It all starts with our household trash. Anything from egg cartons, to broken bug screens, to junk mail can be used to make something useful. Use this site to search for recycling projects that members of the community have shared. Or, if you have an idea, create a profile and add to your heart's content. Let's work together to make the earth a cleaner place.
